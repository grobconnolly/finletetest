FAQ

Answers before you invest

Everything you need to know about Finlete offerings, dividends, risk, and Regulation CF.

We sell athlete-specific preferred stock, a class of preferred stock issued by Finlete Funding, Inc. (or an athlete-specific subsidiary). Each offering's offering circular describes the exact security on sale.

A future earnings contract is an agreement where one party receives capital in exchange for a percentage of the other party's future earnings. In Finlete's case, the covered earnings include salaries, performance bonuses, and other income directly received from the team or league. Expressly excluded are sponsorships, endorsements, investments, or any other off-the-field income.

Dividends are paid twice per year: within 30 days after the All-Star Game and within 30 days after the conclusion of the World Series. Payments begin once the athlete starts generating MLB earnings that Finlete is entitled to collect.

Athletes typically enter future earnings contracts for two reasons:
  1. Investment in their career. Upfront capital lets them access better training, coaching, nutrition, and recovery — improving their odds of reaching the majors.
  2. Fans join their journey. A committed community of investor-fans deepens the athlete's support network, financially and emotionally.

Regulation CF allows ordinary investors to invest in startups and early-growth companies. Unlike Kickstarter, you are not buying a product — you are buying a piece of a company and helping it grow.

Accredited investors can invest as much as they want. If you are not accredited, your limit depends on your annual income or net worth, whichever is greater. If that figure is below $124,000, you may invest 5% of it. If both income and net worth exceed $124,000, your limit is 10%.

Add up all of your assets and subtract all of your liabilities (excluding the value of your primary residence). The resulting sum is your net worth.

We cannot give tax advice. Please consult your accountant or tax advisor before making an investment.

Any individual over 18 years of age can invest.

Yes. Early-stage investments are speculative. The earlier you invest, the greater the risk. If a young company fails, your ownership interest could lose all value. Dilution, limited voting power, and multi-year illiquidity are all possible. Startup investments should only form part of a balanced, overall portfolio.

The preferred stock of Finlete Funding, Inc. is not publicly traded. The primary return on these offerings comes through dividends tied to the athlete's future earnings. The company may eventually build an alternative trading system allowing share transfers, but this is not guaranteed.

Shares sold via Regulation Crowdfunding are subject to a one-year lockup. During that period they may only be transferred in limited circumstances — typically to the issuing company, an accredited investor, or a family member (including in cases of death or divorce).

If the minimum funding target is not reached, all funds are returned to investors after the close of the offering.

All disclosure documents — including the Form C and offering circular — are linked from each athlete's offering page.

You can cancel your investment at any time, for any reason, until 48 hours prior to a closing. If your funds are already in escrow, they will be promptly refunded. To cancel, email info@dealmakersecurities.com.

At minimum, the company files annual reports with the SEC, including certified financial statements, available 120 days after the fiscal year end. These are posted on the company's website.

After an offering closes, Finlete Funding may continue its relationship with DealMaker Securities for future offerings. DealMaker's affiliates may also provide ongoing services. There is no guarantee any services will continue after the offering ends.
