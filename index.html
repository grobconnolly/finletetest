FAQs

Answers before you invest

A quick primer on how Finlete offerings work. For the full list, see our FAQ page.

You are buying preferred stock in an entity (Finlete Funding, Inc. or an athlete-specific subsidiary) that has contracted to receive a percentage of a named athlete's future MLB earnings. Every share entitles you to a fixed fraction of those collected earnings.

A future earnings contract is an agreement where one party receives capital in exchange for giving the other party a percentage of their future earnings. In Finlete's case, the covered earnings include salaries, performance bonuses, and other income directly received from the team or league — not sponsorships, endorsements, investments, or any off-the-field income.

Dividends are paid twice per year: within 30 days after the All-Star Game, and within 30 days after the conclusion of the World Series — but only once the named athlete begins generating MLB earnings for Finlete to collect.

Upfront capital lets prospects invest in their own development — better training, coaching, nutrition, and recovery — without financial stress. It also connects them to a community of investor-fans who are literally rooting for their success.
